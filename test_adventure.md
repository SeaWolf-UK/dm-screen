**The Five-Foot Passage — Terrain Rules**

| FIVE-FOOT PASSAGE — TACTICAL CONSTRAINTS |
| :---- |
| **The tunnel is five feet wide. This has the following mechanical effects:** •  No flanking is possible. Only one attacker can be adjacent to a defender at any time. •  No formations. The party fights in a column. Only the front player is in melee range. •  The rear guard have Pack Tactics active (two of them are always adjacent to the front target). The players do not. •  Ranged attacks from behind the front player: DC 10 DEX save or hit a friendly in the front rank. Sharpshooter feat negates this. **CLEVER PLAY BEATS THIS:** •  Smoke or obscurement: rear guard without darkvision are Blinded. DC 10 Athletics to push through them while they are disoriented. •  Light withdrawal: draw them toward the Arteries junction where there is space. The five-foot advantage evaporates in an open room. •  Holding the chokepoint: one durable defender can hold the passage while casters attack from behind. Pack Tactics stops working if the rear guard can only reach one player at a time. |

| TACTICAL NOTES This is a resource drain encounter, not a dramatic combat. The tunnel is supposed to be unpleasant. That is the point. Players with area spells (Thunderwave, Burning Hands) can clear the tunnel in one or two casts if they accept the five-foot passage limitation. The confined space makes AOE extremely efficient — the rear guard cannot spread out. After the fight: they emerge from the tunnel mouth. The city wall behind them. Above the Drips, the sky is orange where it should be dark. Ahead: firelight between standing stones. |
| :---- |

| OUTCOMES & CONSEQUENCES Standard fight: five rounds maximum, rear guard neutralised. Resources spent feed directly into Beat Three difficulty. Clever play: smoke, light manipulation, drawing them out. Faster, cheaper. Reward it. Cannot be bypassed. The players go through this room. |

| TEN-ROUND CLOCK |
| :---- |
